## 1. The Nation

**Azzadah** is an orc-ruled nation. Ritualistic, brutal society.

### Orc anatomy (signature trait)
- Penis is **horse-shaped and horse-sized** — equine length, equine girth, equine taper.
- Base of shaft has a **canine-style knot** that locks once seated past the partner's sphincter.
- **The knot inflates — biologically closer to real canine bulbus glandis:**
  - **At rest:** knot is *present* as a noticeable bulge at the base of the shaft, but only *partially* engorged. Maybe half its full size. Visible when the cock is exposed but not yet at locking dimensions.
  - **During the rut:** stimulation causes inflation to begin. By end of rut, knot is larger than rest but not yet at full size.
  - **At seating:** orc drives the partially-inflated knot through the sphincter. Still brutal — the half-size knot is still enormous — but not the rim-shredding catastrophe a rigid full-size insertion would be.
  - **Inside her — the lock-in mechanism:** knot continues inflating after seating. **The expansion is what seals her**, not the rim snapping closed behind a rigid object. It swells inside her until too large for the rim to release.
  - **At maximum:** knot at full size, rim dilated around its widest point, seal complete.
  - **Withdrawal:** orc has biological control over deflation. To withdraw he *deflates* the knot back to rest size, then can pull out. This is how the §7 voiding works — controlled deflation, not rigid extraction.
- The **§6d reactive feedback loop** fits cleanly: the knot inflates *further* in response to captive's involuntary spasms. Capable of additional growth past baseline maximum when stimulated.
- **Sadistic misuse** (§9c popping death, Vorgath's popping fuck): orc deliberately *fails to deflate* before withdrawal. Pulls the knot out at full inflation. This is what produces the catastrophic damage and the wet *pop* sound. The popping is engineered misuse of the inflation system.

## 2. Sheathing — the core ritual

**Sheathing** is the practice of capturing a human female and binding her permanently to an orc via anal knot-lock. She is strapped to the orc and **never released**. The bond is for life.

### Function (open — see §6)
Not yet pinned down whether sheathing is:
- Punishment of captured females
- Status display / trophy for the orc
- Religious / sacramental rite
- Some mix
Current lean: **sacramental** — the structure of the ritual (working phase + sealing climax) reads ceremonial, not transactional. To confirm.

### Survival
Sustained — sheathed females are meant to live indefinitely in the bonded state. Survival of the act itself depends on orc skill (see §4). Long-term survival depends on the captive's temperament (see §5).

## 3. The ritual — phenomenology

### Phase 1: Restraint and presentation
Captive is bound bent-over, secured at wrists, waist, thighs, ankles. Zero leverage. She has been converted from a person into a fixture before any contact occurs. The positioning itself is part of the breaking.

### Phase 2: The rut (working phase)
- Press, breach, shaft entry all happen rapidly — orc is working, not pacing for the lock.
- Full depth, full withdrawal, repeat. Each cycle re-tears clotting tissue.
- **Knot is at the base of the shaft the entire time**, slapping against her exterior on every outstroke. She feels it. She knows it's coming.
- Orc-paced rhythm — non-human force and tempo. She is being used as a sleeve.
- Wet/percussive sound. Blood, mucus, his lubricant, sweat. She voids. She vomits. The rig holds her through all of it.
- Time collapses for her. Could be ten minutes, could be an hour.
- Distinct horror layer: she knows the knot is coming. The rut is hell with worse hell visibly approaching.

**Ritual function of the rut:** the orcs are working her open enough that the knot will seat without catastrophic tearing. Without the rut, the knot kills. With it, the rim is softened/blooded/dilated enough to survive the seal.

### Phase 3: The seal (knot insertion + inflation)
- Orc's rhythm shifts — slow windup or frenzy, depends on the individual.
- He drives in deep and stops. Shaft fully seated, not moving.
- The slick *partially-inflated* knot is pushed past the sphincter at its half-size state. Still brutal — still tearing — but survivable for normal-frame captives.
- Knot now inside her, beginning to inflate further.
- **The seal completes via inflation, not via rim-snap.** Inside her, the knot continues swelling. She feels the *growth* — pressure expanding outward, rim being forced wider from the inside. The expansion is the lock.
- Petite captives (e.g. Vorgath's preferences) die at this stage when the inflation crosses the threshold their bodies can survive.

### Phase 4: Lock-in — the swelling that won't stop
- After the knot is seated, she feels it *begin to grow.* Small at first. Then more.
- Her body registers the *expansion* — pressure building outward, rim being forced wider from the inside.
- She has time to *understand it as growth* before it locks. **She knows it's growing. She feels the inflation in real time.**
- She tries to pull away — the swelling continues — she is becoming sealed by the swelling itself.
- Realization arrives as **a sustained understanding rather than a snap event.** She has seconds of internal expansion to register:
  - *it is still growing.*
  - *it is still growing.*
  - *it will not stop growing.*
  - *when it stops growing, it will be too large to come out.*
- When the inflation finally plateaus, the lock is complete. The rim is held wide open from within.
- **The comprehension keening begins DURING the inflation, not after.** She starts wailing as the expansion crosses the threshold from "pain" into "permanent." The keen rises in pitch with the swelling — pressure increases, wail intensifies, comprehension lands incrementally over the inflation period.
- **This makes the keening MORE psychologically grueling than the old rigid-pop version.** She has *time to know* she is being locked in, in real time, as the locking happens. The old version was a sudden trap; this version is a *closing trap she watches close.*
- Failure modes here: catatonia, dissociative amnesia for everything Before, psychotic break.

### Phase 5: First minute after
Hyperventilation, uncontrollable shaking, tears/snot/drool/often vomit. Every orc movement drives the foreign mass against everything just traumatized. She learns in this minute that *his body moving* is now her physics. The strapping/rigging happens while she's in this state; by the time she's coherent the rig is set and the ritual is over. She missed her own induction because her nervous system was elsewhere.

### Vocal progression through the knot moment

The sounds she makes during knot insertion + lock-in are their own arc. Craft detail for renders and prose.

**Just before (he stops moving)**

**Pop-through (knot past the rim, snap closed)**

**Craft anchors**
- *Register progression* is the spine: high pleading → high scream → cracked broken scream → silent open-mouth → comprehension wail → keening → flat monotone. Each is a different *sound*, not just different volume.
- **Silences** matter as much as screams (pop-through silence; open-mouth silent scream). They break the expected pattern; higher impact than the loudest moments.
- **Comprehension wail vs pain scream** is the most useful distinction for prose. Pain scream = body alone. Wail = mind included. Landing this distinction is what shows the captive's *humanity* surviving the pain — making her suffering land as a person's, not a body's.

## 5. The captive's experience (post-seal arc)

### Hours 1–24 — Shock
Disbelief loop. Brain refuses the new permanent state. Adrenaline, hyperventilation, dissociative flickers. Most screaming happens here.

### Days 2–7 — Bargaining and breaking
She tries everything — pleading, going limp, fighting, holding still, offering things, refusing food. None of it changes anything. Most captives fracture here. Some go catatonic. Some develop frantic anticipatory compliance: every orc movement hurts her, so his mood becomes her weather.

### Weeks 2–8 — Adaptation or collapse

- **Breakage:** dissociate permanently. Body keeps functioning. Person inside checks out. Eyes go flat. Useful as a trophy, useless as a person.

### Months in — Bonded states
Trauma-bonding territory. Not love — something uglier. Sustained proximity + total dependence + no exit means his voice becomes the only safety signal her nervous system has, even though he is the threat. Relief when he returns, panic when he's gone (she can't drink/shift/piss without him). Some captives start to identify with the orc's status — *his* trophy, *his* prize — because being his something is the only identity left.

### Years in — Survivors ("settled")
Not human anymore in the way she was. Knows his body better than her own. May whisper to him in his sleep. May hate him with perfect clarity and still adjust the strap when it bites, because the alternative is worse. Orc-society probably has a name for this state — proposed: **"settled"** sheath. To confirm/name.

### Branch points across captives
- **Pre-capture temperament** drives outcome most. Defiant types break hardest/fastest. Pragmatic types adapt and survive longest. Pious types may reframe as divine ordeal and become eerie.
- **Sole vs one-of-many** — sole sheath produces intense codependence; one of many produces competitive, jealous, status-anxious bond shapes.
- **Whether the orc speaks to her** — silence breaks people differently than abuse-with-conversation.

## 6a. Family-sheathing — village raids

When Azzadah orcs raid a village, **whole families are sheathed together.** Father, mother, sons, daughters — taken as a unit.

### Raid mechanics
- Unit of capture is the family, not the individual.
- Implies adjacent hostile frontier: Azzadah border human territory, raid bands cross it, human villages know they are targets.
- Plot fuel: refugee dynamics, frontier fortifications, repeatedly-rebuilt human settlements, possibly a class of human resistance or border fort.

### Why family-sheathing (cultural function — likely all three at once)
- **Trophy** — capturing an entire bloodline is showier than capturing individuals. Family-as-set has display value greater than the sum.
- **Sacramental** — converting a whole lineage in one stroke is ritually significant. Nothing of the family persists outside Azzadah.
- **Designed cruelty** — orcs understand families suffering together is worse than apart. The compound is the point.

### Compound psychological layer (vs solo captives)
- Each family member's trauma is shaped by witnessing what happened to the others. Father watches wife rutted before the seal. Mother sees daughters broken in. Siblings watch each other.
- Empathy becomes a torture vector. Hearing your daughter scream through her seal, locked in your own, breaks something the solo arc doesn't reach.
- **§5 trauma-bond arc warps differently:**
  - Solo captive — old identity erodes with no anchor; orc fills the vacuum (anchor erosion).
  - Family captive — old identity is *actively demolished in front of her* as she watches her family's identity die alongside her own (anchor demolition).
- Long-term proximity (depending on distribution canon) means watching your settled mother adjust her rig at her orc's footstool, in year five — ongoing trauma layer with no end.

### Generational trophy potential
If a captured family includes adult daughters who survive decades, the orc clan holds a **lineage** of sheaths from one bloodline. "Three generations of the Lindwald women." High-prestige clan achievement. Possibly religious distinction.

## 6d. The reactive-knot feedback loop

A load-bearing mechanic across all sealed states. Sheaths, vessels under seal, double-knotted captives — all subject to it.

### The loop
1. Captive's body convulses (any source — pain, sob, gag, shock spasm, hyperventilation, pelvic-floor reflex, body-betrayal arousal contraction, hypnic jerk, internal cramping, temperature regulation spasm, cardiac stress, etc.).
2. Convulsion grips the orc's shaft and knot — same muscle pattern as willing orgasm, here pain-driven and involuntary.
3. Grip stimulates the knot, which **grows.**
4. Growing knot creates new pain at the rim and new internal pressure.
5. New pain triggers another convulsion.
6. Loop.

### What this means structurally
- **The captive's pain is self-amplifying.** Her own body is the engine of additional torment.
- **The orc does nothing.** His knot is the apparatus. Her body operates it. He can sleep, eat, talk, fight — the device runs through her involuntary reactions regardless.
- **He doesn't even have to be conscious.** The torture continues during his sleep, autopilot, neglect-during-atrocity.

### Triggers (continuous, multiple sources)
- Pain spasms.
- Shock-state seizures.
- Sob-spasms.
- Gag and retch reflexes.
- Hyperventilation chest-spasms.
- Pelvic-floor expel reflex.
- Body-betrayal arousal contractions.
- Lurching restraint reflex.
- Hypnic jerks during exhausted half-sleep.
- Internal cramping (gas, impaction).
- Temperature regulation rigors.
- Cardiac stress palpitations.

All involuntary. None stop because she wants them to. Every one feeds the knot.

### Captive's experience of the loop
First hours: doesn't know about the loop. Just hurts more whenever her body moves.

Hour 4–5: some captives figure it out.
> *when I move — when I — I — I'm doing it. I'm doing it to myself.*
> *(another spasm at the realization)*
> *(more pain)*

The recognition is its own horror. **She has discovered that her own body is collaborating with her abuser without her consent.** Only way to stop the loop is perfect stillness — impossible because her body is convulsing on its own.

### Why dissociation is structurally selected for
- Captives who try desperate conscious stillness make spasms worse (locking-then-releasing in larger contractions).
- Only reliable way to stop reacting is **to dissociate** — let the mind leave the body so the body can go limp.
- This is one of the structural reasons sheaths develop dissociative coping arcs (§5). Not just psychological shelter — physical pain-reduction.

### Orc-culture implications

- **Reactive captives milk more.** Some orcs prefer captives who spasm — more pleasure delivered, especially overnight.
- Fresh sheaths are *better* on the reactivity metric. Veteran sheaths who have learned stillness deliver less stimulation.
- **The orc-preference paradox:** reactive captives give more pleasure but die faster (loop damages them more). Still captives are sustainable but blander. Different orc temperaments prefer different captives.
- **First-night fresh sheaths are prized for this.** Some orcs may rotate fresh captives specifically for new-sheath nights and reassign veteran captives elsewhere.

## 15. The corrected canon of the Keen

The Keen is **animal lament,** not music. Bodies producing the sound of comprehended permanent loss. Wet, broken, repetitive, primal. Like the sound a person makes when told their entire family is dead — except the captive is sustaining it for minutes because the comprehension keeps landing.
